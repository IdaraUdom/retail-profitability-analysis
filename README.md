**Project Overview**


Discount SuperSavers, a national discount retailer, operates across both online and physical store channels. Following an unsatisfactory annual profitability performance where high revenue volumes did not translate into strong profitability,  stakeholders commissioned an analysis to better understand the factors influencing profit margin outcomes. The objective of this analysis is to identify where profitability and value are created, where profit margins are eroded and which factors have the strongest impact on profitability.

**Analytical Approach**


I led an end-to-end analysis covering data preparation, exploratory analysis, insight developments and data visualization for stakeholders. 

**Analytical Methods Used**

Product and category level profitability analysis to identify value concentration
Discount elasticity analysis to assess the relationship between discount depth, revenue, and margin
Regional analysis to identify performance variations across states
Customer segmentation using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis
Customer-level profitability analysis to evaluate revenue quality


**Key Findings**

**Profitability in Product and Category is Highly Concentrated**   

A clear profitability hierarchy emerges at the category-level. Technology leads due to a small subset of high-revenue, high-margin SKUs. Office Supplies struggles to consistently turn revenue to profit, indicating margin pressure.

**Discounting Erodes Profit Margins Without Driving Sustainable Revenue Volume**   

The current discount strategy structurally erodes profitability without creating worthwhile revenue gains. As discounts increase, profit margins shrink turning into outright profit loss.

**Regional Profitability Performance is Driven by a Small Subset of Outliers**

In profitability, California, New York and Washington lead the pack. The three states account for approximately 41% of total revenue with California alone bringing in approximately 20% and signaling overreliance on these dominant states.

 **Revenue Volume is Not a Reliable Indicator of Customer Segment Value**

Despite leading in revenue volume, the Consumer segment had the lowest profit margin of all customer segments. In contrast, the Home Office had the lowest revenue volume but had the highest profit margin. This suggests that revenue volume is not a reliable indicator of segment performance.

**Customer Value is Unevenly Distributed Across Market Segments**

To understand each segment’s value concentration, customers were grouped using RFM analysis based on their order frequency, recency and revenue contribution. This revealed that untapped customers (low engagement, low spend) represented the largest revenue share but the lowest revenue quality. Platinum customers were few but highly valuable.

 **High-Revenue Accounts Display Profitability Divergence**

Introducing profitability as an additional assessment layer provides clearer insight into revenue quality. A small subset of customers generated substantial revenue volumes with low profitability, and in one case delivering profit loss despite having the highest revenue volume of the group.  

**Strategic Recommendations**

**Reorient Performance Measurement Towards Revenue Quality and Refocus Performance Management on Profitability**
 
Introduce KPIs targeting profitability alongside existing revenue KPIs at the category, segment and account levels. This ensures new growth initiatives will prioritize revenue quality over volume to increase profitability. 

**Establish Guardrails Around Discounting Strategy**

Setting formal standard discount threshold caps at regional, category and customer levels can limit profit margin erosion while preserving revenue volumes. Formal caps allow Discount SuperSavers to preserve customer value perception while also keeping the business’ commercial model. 

**Prioritize Revenue Quality Within Growth Efforts**
Within identified high-revenue customer segments and markets, introducing targeted initiatives aimed at improving engagement and order frequency could shift customers from stagnancy or low-value to higher-value customer segments and markets.

**Protect and Scale Identified Profit Drivers**
 
The high-performing SKUs, regions and customer accounts identified in the analysis represent scalable profit centers and opportunities. Prioritizing availability, pricing consistency and operational focus around these profit drivers can reduce reliance on under-performing areas


**Estimated Business Impact**

Initiating formal standard discount threshold caps could reduce margin erosion whilst preserving revenue volume. A shift towards prioritizing profitability focused KPIs could lead to more informed strategic and commercial decision-making. In conjunction, prioritizing revenue quality could lead to increased stable profitability without needing to grow revenue volume. Finally, improving under-performing regions and customer accounts can reduce performance volatility while also preventing over-reliance on narrow subsets.
